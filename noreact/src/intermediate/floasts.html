Venus is the second planet from the Sun in our solar system, located between Mercury and Earth. It is often referred to as Earth's "sister planet" because it is similar in size and composition to Earth. Here are some key characteristics of Venus: Size and Composition: Venus has a diameter of approximately 12,104 kilometers (7,521 miles), which is very close to Earth's size. It is composed primarily of rock and metal and has a dense, rocky surface. Atmosphere: Venus has a thick and dense atmosphere composed mainly of carbon dioxide (about 96.5%) with traces of nitrogen and other gases. The atmosphere creates a strong greenhouse effect, trapping heat and making Venus one of the hottest planets in the solar system. Surface Features: Venus has a heavily cratered highland region and vast plains covered by volcanic features, including large shield volcanoes and extensive lava flows. The planet's surface is extremely inhospitable due to its high temperatures, crushing atmospheric pressure, and acidic clouds.
